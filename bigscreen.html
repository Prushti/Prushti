BIG SCREEN is a mobile application to book movie tickets. With this application users can book tickets, read movie information,search upcoming movies,select desired seats and make payment with minimum steps. ​

View Mobile Prototype

The Problem

People are unsatisfied with less functional and less compatible movie apps. Users want movie mobile apps from where they can select seats with less additional fees. They need a product which provides all information related to movies like upcoming movie trailers, movie recommendation, trending movies, user review, critic’s review, seat selection and secure payment methods all at one place.

The Solution

To solve these problems, I created a mobile application to book movie tickets by keeping the best user experience in mind. With this application users can search current and upcoming movies in nearest theater, see user and critic review related particular movie, book desired show with seat selection. This user friendly application is designed to make the life of all users easy regardless of their demography.

The Discovery

To know if there is a potential market for such prodcuts, I needed to gather insights about the target market from both ends of the user.

Market Confirmation


There is consumer demand for more functionalize products for movie ticket booking. Big Screen provides the platform for customers to book tickets.


67% of participants use movie ticket booking applications.

35% use mobile app twice in a week to book movie tickets.

25% use more than one ticket booking application.

45% want to improve apps by adding more functional features.

65% thinks booking online save time, save money and have more options.


User personas

To gain a deeper understanding of my potential users, I interviewed them as they talked about their goals and frustrations. Not only did I confirm some of the survey results, but I also discovered that this pool of audience heavily values user-friendly designs — to the point where they determine whether to keep using an app or not within the first few minutes of the product experience.


Throughout the project, I continuously referred back to these personas to guide design decisions and priorities and to ensure that I was always thinking from their shoes, addressing their goals and frustrations.

Competitive Analysis

To see where BIG SCREEN would stand in the market, I researched 3 other tickets booking service products (Fandango, Atom, and AMC), registered an account for each, and analyzed how BIG SCREEN would differentiate itself.

The Strategy

Information Architecture, User Stories, User Flows


With the product concept and personas in mind, I began constructing the mobile sitemap, which laid the foundation and structure of BIG SCREEN. Followed by listing out all possible actions that make the MVP a truly user-friendly, time-saving, intuitive, and functional mobile app, I was able to prioritize and eliminate user stories while spotting issues in user flows and functionalities. For me, this part of the process reinforced the product concept, scopes, and goals of the app.

The Framework

Starting with wireframes, I was able to explore all of my ideas. I didn’t have to worry about anything else but the structure and how content was going to be laid out. I worked on refining the hand-drawn sketch, focused on hierarchy, design elements, as well as placements. I created multiple layouts for the same screen and asked for feedback. Although paper wireframes can communicate a whole lot by themselves, creating a mid-fidelity wireframe helped me gather more insightful feedback from potential users. I believe it is important never to ask my users to imagine things because it can someimes go in a very different direction.



Branding and Style Guide


Logo Design

The BIG SCREEN logo is minimal symbol design with the movie screen concept. This powerful symbol represents the Movie ticket booking experience.


Color Palette

BIG SCREEN uses minimal colors like red as the main color palette and shades of blue,light green and orange as a secondary color. It shows the luxury feeling for the users.

Typography

Montserrat is a geometric sans-serif typeface representative of the aesthetics, very simple and universal. It has an appearance of efficiency and forwardness in the design. It allows us to emphasize the voices on BIG SCREEN.

The Refinement

Usability tests and metrics revealed that some of the process and design decisions I made were not as intuitive as I thought. Referring back to the personas, I knew that users heavily value a user-friendly product and would like to minimize the time spent on understanding it. As a result, I needed to iterate, test, and achieve measurable outcomes to overcome these product shortcomings.

Conclusion

Surprisingly every user that tested my app said it was intuitive and they really liked it a lot. They were impressed by how natural the experience was great overall. There were some things asked to be added that I feel would be a feature to implement in the future such as events ticket booking, live concert shows ticket booking.


I had doubts about the ability to find a problem within this application, but interestingly enough as I did more competitive analysis I realized there is a lot of room to play within this market. And plenty of areas to improve on for that matter.


I think what I would have done differently is made sure to make a more fully fledged prototype that had conditional logic for certain paths people would have taken to make it even that much more natural.


On a more serious note I learned more about the process of user research which was incredible. I’m thankful to have had 35 people to take my test. I feel I had a considerable amount of data to sift through and I really enjoyed finding key insights from this data.
